Community Asset Tracking Systems

Community asset tracking refers to the implementation of digital technologies to monitor and manage physical resources in local or remote communities. These systems are particularly valuable in developing nations or island nations where logistics and oversight are challenged by geographic isolation.

Technologies Used

  • RFID: Used for passive tracking of inventory or equipment.
  • GPS: Enables real-time location tracking of mobile assets.
  • Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE): Suitable for short-range tracking.
  • QR codes or Barcodes: Cost-effective methods for non-electronic tagging.

Case Example: Kiribati

In island nations such as Kiribati, asset tracking is being explored for applications such as managing shared tools, solar equipment, and community-owned bicycles. Internet connectivity challenges are addressed by using offline-compatible systems such as local mobile apps or peer-to-peer syncing.

Benefits

  • Reduces loss and theft of public or shared property
  • Improves accountability and transparency
  • Streamlines community project management

Challenges

  • Initial cost of RFID or GPS hardware
  • Internet access and power supply limitations
  • Need for training and digital literacy
